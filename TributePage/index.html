Quaid-e-Azam

"Think a hundred times before you take a decision, but once that decision is taken, stand by it as one man." - Muhammad Ali Jinnah

  • DECEMBER 25, 1876

    Born in Karachi, Sindh, British India, to a wealthy Gujarati family.

  • 1906

    Joined the Indian National Congress.

  • 1913

    Started his legal practice in Bombay.

  • 1920

    Joined the All India Muslim League.

  • 1929

    Served as the President of the All India Muslim League.

  • 1930

    Played a vital role in the struggle for the independence of Pakistan.

  • 1940

    Delivered the historic "Pakistan Resolution" at Lahore.

  • 1947

    Pakistan was declared as an independent nation.

  • 1948

    Served as the first Governor-General of Pakistan.

  • September 11, 1948

    Passed away in Karachi, Pakistan.

"Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was a visionary leader who inspired a nation. His unwavering determination and strategic brilliance led to the creation of Pakistan, a homeland for millions. Through his eloquent speeches and resolute actions, he ignited a spirit of hope and unity among the Muslim populace of India. Just as Stephen Hawking captivated the world with the mysteries of the cosmos, Quaid-e-Azam captured hearts with the dream of a sovereign nation. Both men were exceptional leaders who left an enduring legacy of inspiration."
